## Competitor Intelligence Report — Proventia Oy

## 1. Formal Certification Framework

### Governing Body & Legal Basis

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Issuing Authority** | Flag State Administration (via Recognized Organizations acting on its behalf) |
| **Primary Legal Instrument** | MARPOL Annex VI, Regulation 13 (Nitrogen Oxides) |
| **Technical Standard** | NOx Technical Code 2008 (resolution MEPC.177(58)), as amended by MEPC.251(66) |
| **SCR-Specific Guidelines** | MEPC.198(62) (2011); MEPC.291(71) (2017); MEPC.399(83) (2025) |
| **EGCS Equivalence Basis** | MARPOL Annex VI, Regulation 4 (Equivalents) |
| **EGCS Technical Guidelines** | MEPC.259(68) (2015 EGCS Guidelines), replaced/updated by MEPC.289(71) (2017) |

The NOx Technical Code 2008 is **mandatory** under MARPOL Annex VI Regulation 13. It governs testing, survey, and certification of all marine diesel engines >130 kW installed on ships subject to Annex VI.

**Key result document**: Engine International Air Pollution Prevention (EIAPP) Certificate — issued per engine, not per ship. For SCR-fitted systems, one EIAPP covers the "engine + SCR" as an integrated system (per 2011/2017 SCR Guidelines).

## 2. Step-by-Step Approval Workflow

### 2A. Engine Modification / Retrofitted Engine Pathway (Engine + SCR)

**Step 1 — Pre-Certification Application**
- Applicant (typically engine manufacturer or specialized retrofit company) submits to the Flag State Administration (or its RO) a complete Technical File covering the "engine + SCR" system.
- File must include: reductant type/concentration, injection system specs, catalyst block specifications/arrangement, allowable exhaust gas temperature range (min/max), pressure loss (Δp), deterioration rates, and reductant slip minimization measures.
- For Scheme B (see below), scaled SCR chamber validation data must be included if applicable.

**Step 2 — Certification Scheme Selection**
- **Scheme A** (preferred): Engine and SCR tested together on a test bed per Chapter 5 of NOx Technical Code 2008. Used when technically feasible (typically smaller engines).
- **Scheme B** (used for large/specialized engines): Engine and SCR certified separately. Involves engine test-bed baseline measurement, SCR modeling, and mandatory on-board confirmation test at 25%, 50%, and 75% rated power. The on-board test must confirm NOx reduction efficiency within 5% of Technical File values.

**Step 3 — Test Bed Testing (Scheme A) or Engine Baseline Testing (Scheme B)**
- For Scheme A: Full test cycle per NTC 2008 Appendix II (test cycles: E2, E3, D2, or C1 depending on engine application).
- For Scheme B: Engine NOx baseline established per NTC 2008; then SCR reduction modeling submitted; on-board confirmation test conducted before the vessel enters service.

**Step 4 — Technical File Approval**
- Administration reviews and approves the Technical File for "engine + SCR" as a combined system.
- One Technical File, one EIAPP certificate for the combined system.

**Step 5 — Pre-Installation Survey (Pre-Certification Survey)**
- Conducted at engine builder's works before installation.
- Verifies the engine as built conforms to the approved Technical File.

**Step 6 — Initial Certification Survey (Post-Installation)**
- Conducted after engine is installed on board.
- For Scheme B: Cannot be completed until the on-board confirmation test demonstrates compliant reduction efficiency.
- Includes verification of SCR installation, reductant storage and injection system, catalyst beds, monitoring and alarm systems.

**Step 7 — EIAPP Certificate Issuance**
- Certificate issued to the specific engine, noting the NOx Tier (III), applicable test cycle, rating, and "engine + SCR" configuration.
- The vessel's International Air Pollution Prevention (IAPP) Certificate is updated at the initial survey to reflect the new engine's NOx status.

### 2B. EGCS/Scrubber Pathway (SOx + Potential NOx)

For EGCS used as an **equivalent** under Regulation 4 (MARPOL Annex VI):

**Step 1 — Equivalent Application**
- Owner/applicant submits to Flag State or RO requesting approval of EGCS as an alternative compliance method.
- Basis: MEPC.259(68) (2015 EGCS Guidelines) / MEPC.289(71) (2017) — technical standards and verification procedures.
- Note: EGCS primarily addresses **SOx**, not NOx. For NOx, an SCR is required. An EGCS alone does NOT satisfy Tier III NOx requirements.

**Step 2 — Technical Assessment**
- EGCS must meet the monitoring, alarm, emergency shutdown, redundancy, and material suitability requirements defined in the guidelines.
- Plan review and survey by RO/class.

**Step 3 — Onboard Testing & Survey**
- Functional testing, wash water discharge compliance verification, monitoring system verification.

**Step 4 — Certificate Issuance**
- EGCS Type Approval Certificate (class) + Updated IAPP Certificate noting the equivalent arrangement.

## 4. ENG CII and SEEMP Documentation Requirements Alongside NOx Certification

### CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator) — **NOT directly linked to NOx certification**

- CII (operational carbon intensity) is governed by MARPOL Annex VI **Chapter 4** (Energy Efficiency), **Regulation 28** — separate from NOx Regulation 13.
- Ships ≥ 5,000 GT must have an **attained CII** calculated annually and hold a **CII Certificate** (A, B, C, D, or E rating).
- **CII is independent of NOx Tier certification** — different legal framework, different certificate.

### SEEMP (Ship Energy Efficiency Management Plan) — **Partially linked**

- **SEEMP Part I** (overall energy efficiency management) is required under Regulation 26 — separate from NOx.
- **SEEMP Part II** (Data Collection Plan for DCS) — required for ships ≥ 5,000 GT — separate from NOx.
- **SEEMP Part III** (enhanced SHIELD regime for ships with CII rating D for 3+ consecutive years or E in any year) — separate from NOx.

### NOx-Specific Documentation Required Onboard (per NOx Technical Code 2008)

| Document | Requirement Source | Description |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **EIAPP Certificate** | Regulation 13 / NTC 2008 | Engine-specific; states NOx Tier, test cycle, rating, and any SCR configuration |
| **Technical File** | NTC 2008 Reg. 2.3.4 | All NOx-critical components, settings, operating values, allowable adjustment ranges, parent engine test data, spare parts |
| **Record Book of Engine Parameters** | NTC 2008 Reg. 6.2.2.8 | Records all replacements/changes to NOx-critical components, settings, and operating values |
| **NOx operational compliance log** | Regulation 13/5.3 (per Administration) | Records Tier status, on/off status of NOx abatement equipment, date/time/position of ECA entry/exit |

### Key Takeaway
**ENG CII and SEEMP documentation are NOT required as part of NOx Tier III certification itself.** They are parallel requirements under the Energy Efficiency chapter of MARPOL Annex VI. However, ships subject to Tier III NOx rules (i.e., operating in ECAs) are very likely also subject to CII/EEXI/SEEMP requirements given their size (typically ≥ 400 GT, usually ≥ 5,000 GT for EEXI/CII).

## 5. Timeline from Application to Certification

### Indicative Timeline for a Single Engine SCR Retrofit (Scheme B)

| Phase | Activity | Duration |
|-------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Application preparation (Technical File compilation, SCR modeling) | 2 – 6 weeks |
| 2 | RO/Administration review of Technical File | 2 – 4 weeks |
| 3 | Test bed engine testing (Scheme B baseline) | 1 – 2 weeks |
| 4 | SCR system manufacturing/fabrication | 8 – 24 weeks (parallel with testing) |
| 5 | Installation on board vessel | 2 – 6 weeks |
| 6 | Onboard confirmation test (Scheme B mandatory test) | 1 – 2 weeks |
| 7 | Survey, documentation, EIAPP/IAPP certificate issuance | 1 – 3 weeks |
| **Total** | **Single engine retrofit** | **3 – 6 months** |

> For a large 2-stroke main engine retrofit with SCR (Scheme B), timelines can extend to **6–9 months**, particularly if the onboard confirmation test reveals deviations requiring rework. MEPC.83 (2025) amendments to the NOx Technical Code (entry into force 1 September 2026) introduce a formal re-certification pathway for retrofitted engines, which may streamline and clarify this process.

### Timeline Drivers / Variables
- Engine size and type (2-stroke vs. 4-stroke; large bore 2-stroke engines often require Scheme B)
- Availability of test facilities
- SCR system delivery lead times
- Shipyard availability for installation
- RO surveyor scheduling
- Completeness of Technical File submission

## 7. Grandfathering Provisions and Exemptions for Older Engines

### 7.1 General Exemptions from NOx Regulation 13

| Exemption Category | Regulatory Basis | Detail |
|-------------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Emergency engines** | Regulation 13.1 | Engines used solely for emergency purposes or solely powering emergency equipment |
| **Engines ≤130 kW** | Regulation 13.1 | Not subject to NOx limits |
| **Lifeboat engines** | Regulation 13.1 | Only while用于 emergency purposes |
| **Voyages within flag state jurisdiction** | Regulation 13.1 | If Administration establishes alternative NOx control measure |
| **Recreational craft <24m** | Regulation 14.3 | Specifically designed and used solely for recreational purposes |
| **Ships <750 kW propulsion** | Regulation 14.3 | Where design/construction limitations prevent compliance |
| **Pre-2021 recreational craft <500 GT, ≥24m** | Regulation 14.3 | Specifically designed and used solely for recreational purposes |

### 7.2 Pre-2000 / Pre-Tier Engines

- Ships constructed **before 1 January 2000** are generally outside the Tier I construction date trigger.
- However, Regulation 13.7 (Retrospective Certification) applies to large engines (>5,000 kW, ≥90 L/cylinder) installed on ships constructed 1 January 1990 – 31 December 1999. These may require **Approved Method** certification to meet Tier I standards.
- Pre-2000 engines not meeting these criteria are generally **not subject** to mandatory NOx Tier certification requirements.

### 7.3 Tier III Retroactive Application

- **Tier III does NOT apply retroactively** to ships based on installation date — only to ships **constructed** on or after the applicable Tier III ECA date.
- A ship constructed in 2014 (Tier II compliant) operating in a Tier III ECA is **not required to meet Tier III** — it operates under its original Tier II certification.
- **Exception**: If a Tier II engine undergoes **substantial modification** on or after 1 January 2016 in a Tier III ECA, it may be re-certified to the Tier applicable at the time of modification.

### 7.4 MEPC.83 (2025) Amendments — Engine Re-Certification Pathway

- MEPC 83 (April 2025) adopted amendments to the NOx Technical Code concerning **re-certification of engines subject to substantial modification** or engines being certified to a Tier they were not originally certified to at time of installation.
- **Entry into force: 1 September 2026** (parties invited to consider early application).
- This formalizes the re-certification pathway for retrofitted engines and is expected to provide greater clarity on the process for upgrading existing engines to Tier III via engine modification or SCR/EGR retrofit.

## 8. EGCS/SCR Retrofit Rules — Specific Distinctions

### 8.1 EGCS (Scrubbers) — SOx Only

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What it reduces** | SOx (primary), PM; **NOx reduction marginal only (~10–20%)** |
| **IMO approval basis** | MARPOL Annex VI, Regulation 4 (Equivalents) + MEPC.259(68) / MEPC.289(71) EGCS Guidelines |
| **NOx compliance** | EGCS alone does **NOT** bring an engine into Tier III compliance |
| **Certification document** | EGCS Type Approval Certificate (class) + Updated IAPP Certificate noting the equivalent arrangement |
| **Scrubber types** | Open loop, closed loop, hybrid; all require wash water monitoring per MEPC.307(73) |
| **Survey** | Plan review, construction survey, commissioning, ongoing annual/intermediate surveys |

**Key point for Proventia**: EGCS/scrubbers address SOx compliance (IMO 2020 sulphur limit) but are not a pathway to NOx Tier III compliance. This is a critical distinction for competitor analysis — companies marketing scrubbers as "emission compliance" solutions for NECA operations are not addressing the NOx challenge.

### 8.2 SCR (Selective Catalytic Reduction) — NOx Primary

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What it reduces** | NOx (up to 90%+ reduction; converts NOx to N₂ and H₂O) |
| **IMO approval basis** | MARPOL Annex VI, Regulation 13 + NOx Technical Code 2008 + MEPC.198(62) / MEPC.291(71) / MEPC.399(83) SCR Guidelines |
| **Certification** | EIAPP Certificate for "engine + SCR" combined system |
| **Schemes** | Scheme A (test bed combined test) or Scheme B (separate testing + onboard confirmation test) |
| **Reductant** | Typically urea solution (40% in water); ammonia injection also possible |
| **Operational risk** | Ammonia slip; catalyst fouling at low loads; requires careful load management |

### 8.3 EGR (Exhaust Gas Recirculation)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What it reduces** | NOx (significant reduction; lowers peak combustion temperature) |
| **IMO approval basis** | Same as SCR — NOx Technical Code 2008 |
| **Key difference from SCR** | In-board modification to air intake/exhaust system; more invasive than SCR |
| **Wash water** | Bleed-off water must meet MEPC.307(73) discharge criteria (<15 ppm oil content) |
| **Fuel penalty** | Yes — reduces combustion efficiency |

### 8.4 Engine Modification vs. After-Treatment — Summary

| Approach | NOx Reduction | Certification Route | Complexity |
|----------|--------------|--------------------|------------|
| **Engine tuning alone** | Moderate (Tier I→II achievable; Tier III unlikely) | Standard EIAPP via NTC 2008 | Low |
| **SCR (after-treatment)** | >90% (Tier III achievable) | NTC 2008 + SCR Guidelines; Scheme A or B | Medium |
| **EGR (after-treatment)** | High (Tier III achievable) | NTC 2008 (similar process to SCR) | High |
| **Dual fuel (Otto cycle)** | Near-zero NOx in gas mode | NTC 2008 (gas mode); reverts to Tier II in liquid fuel mode | Very High |
| **EGCS/Scrubber** | Minimal NOx (~10–20%) | Regulation 4 Equivalent + EGCS Guidelines; **NOT a NOx Tier III solution** | Medium |

### 8.5 Multiple Engine Operational Profiles (MEOP) — MEPC.82/83 Amendment

- MEPC 83 adopted amendments allowing a marine diesel engine to have **multiple engine operational profiles** (MEOP) — i.e., different NOx emission values at different rated power/speed settings.
- Entry into force: **1 March 2027**
- This is relevant for retrofit certification: engines with variable operational profiles (e.g., marine engines with different ratings for different operating modes) can now be certified across all their operational profiles rather than requiring separate certifications for each profile.
